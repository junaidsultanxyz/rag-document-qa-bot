### 1. Pre-lawsuit Rules

**Question:** What must you do before you can sue Discord or start arbitration?

**Answer:** You must try to settle the issue informally. You have to email `disputes@discordapp.com` with your details and problem, then talk with them by phone or video. You can only take formal legal action if the problem is not solved after 60 days.

### 2. Time Limit to Sue

**Question:** How long do you have to bring a legal claim against Discord?

**Answer:** You must start your legal claim within **one (1) year** from when the problem happened.

### 3. Stopping Automatic Arbitration

**Question:** How can you keep your right to a regular trial by a judge or jury?

**Answer:** You must email an opt-out notice to `arbitration-opt-out@discord.com`. You have to do this within **30 days** of September 29, 2025, or within 30 days of making your account, whichever is later.

### 4. Where Lawsuits Happen

**Question:** Where must a U.S. or Canadian lawsuit against Discord be filed?

**Answer:** Any regular lawsuit must be filed strictly in **New Castle County, Delaware**. *(Note: This rule does not apply to European Union consumers).*

### 5. Content Ownership

**Question:** Who owns the text and photos you post on Discord?

**Answer:** You own your content. However, by using Discord, you give the company a free, worldwide license to copy, share, and change your content so they can run and improve their services.

### 6. Large Group Claims

**Question:** What happens if a law firm files 100 or more similar cases against Discord at the same time?

**Answer:** This is treated as a **Mass Filing**. Discord will only handle these cases in groups (batches) of 100 at a time. The other cases must wait their turn.

### 7. Closing an Account

**Question:** Does disabling your Discord account end your legal agreement with them?

**Answer:** **No.** Disabling your account only pauses data processing. To fully end the agreement, you must completely **delete** your account in the settings.

### 8. Rules for Parents

**Question:** Who is responsible if a child buys something or breaks rules on Discord?

**Answer:** The **parent or legal guardian** is fully responsible for everything the child does on the account, including any money spent.

### 9. Lost Data and Outages

**Question:** Is Discord responsible if a server crash deletes your saved messages or pictures?

**Answer:** **No.** Discord does not guarantee continuous service and is not legally responsible for lost data or server outages.

### 10. Data Scraping

**Question:** Can you use a bot or script to gather and sell data from Discord?

**Answer:** **No.** You cannot scrape data or sell information taken from Discord unless you have Discord's official written permission.
